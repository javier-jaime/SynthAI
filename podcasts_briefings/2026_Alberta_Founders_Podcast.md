# **Briefing Document: Insights from Alberta Founders Podcast Episode 1**

## **Executive Summary**

This document synthesizes the key insights, operational challenges, and strategic philosophies discussed in the inaugural episode of the *Alberta Founders Podcast*. The discussion features Hems, founder of Simple French, and Odiri, founder of Disclosy.

The core findings highlight that technical proficiency in product development is insufficient for business success; founders must overcome a natural aversion to sales and marketing to achieve market awareness. A central theme is the "founder's paradox," where the necessity of wearing multiple hats—ranging from accounting to engineering—creates significant mental exhaustion. The document outlines Disclosy’s niche focus on legal tech automation and provides a roadmap for early-stage entrepreneurs, emphasizing that education must be followed by immediate execution.

## **Business Profile: Disclosy**

Disclosy is an automation software platform specifically engineered for the family law sector. Its primary function is to streamline the "financial disclosure" process, which is a mandatory exchange of financial information between separating spouses.

### **Market Positioning and Functionality**

* **Target Audience:** The product is strictly niche, serving family lawyers, family paralegals, and mediators.  
* **Core Utility:** It automates manual workflows, replacing back-and-forth emails and manual document uploads with a centralized system for document movement.  
* **Traceability:** The software focuses on the secure transfer and tracking of documents rather than acting as a "legal detective" or analyzing the content of the documents.

## **Operational Challenges for Early-Stage Founders**

The transition from a "project" to a "business" involves several recurring challenges that founders must navigate to maintain momentum.

### **The "Founder’s Paradox" and Mental Exhaustion**

The most taxing aspect of early-stage entrepreneurship is the requirement to inhabit multiple roles simultaneously. This role-switching is described as mentally exhausting and includes:

* **Product and Engineering:** Building and hosting the application.  
* **Administration:** Managing accounting and ensuring "the numbers are right."  
* **Sales and Marketing:** Generating leads and closing deals.  
* **Support and Social Media:** Managing customer relations and digital presence.

### **The Sales Mandate**

A significant hurdle for technically-minded founders is the necessity of selling. The discussion emphasizes that "selling and building are completely different things."

* **Sales Philosophy:** Effective selling is not about coercion but about identifying if a prospect has a specific problem that the product can fix.  
* **Aversion to Sales:** Founders often wish the need for sales would disappear, yet it remains the "cornerstone" of any business.

### **Managing "Shiny Object Syndrome"**

Founders often face the temptation to build new features based on every customer request. To counter this, the document suggests:

* **Goal Alignment:** Asking if a new task or feature directly contributes to the overall business goal.  
* **Restraint:** Taking a step back when a project begins to deviate from the core mission to avoid building applications that never reach the market.

## **Strategic Market Awareness**

Market awareness is defined as the process of ensuring that the individuals who need a product are aware of its availability.

### **Key Marketing Insights**

* **Product vs. Visibility:** Even the best product in the world will fail if no one knows it exists.  
* **Channel Identification:** Founders must find marketing channels that align with their personality (extroverted vs. introverted) and their specific target market.  
* **Methods of Awareness:** These include advertising, podcast appearances, leveraging personal networks, and word-of-mouth.

### **Future Growth Strategy: Strategic Partnerships**

For Disclosy, the next 12 months are focused on **Strategic B2B Partnerships**.

* **Objective:** To unlock "Ideal Customer Profiles" (ICPs) by collaborating with established entities in the legal space.  
* **Focus Shift:** While the product continues to evolve, the primary energy is now directed toward business development and partnership engagement rather than just internal building.

## **Guidance for First-Time Founders and Students**

The podcast provides actionable advice for those entering the entrepreneurial ecosystem, emphasizing education and the quality of mentorship.

### **The Hierarchy of Advice**

Founders are cautioned to be discerning regarding the advice they accept.

* **"In the Ring":** Advice should only be taken from those who have actual experience in the entrepreneurial journey.  
* **Dismissing Simplistic Solutions:** Suggestions such as "doing everything for free" or "just getting a list and emailing" are often viewed as "BS" because they ignore the complexities of credibility, passion, and financial risk.

### **Execution over Intellectual Property (IP)**

Regarding concerns about IP in the early stages, the consensus is that execution is the primary differentiator. Ideas are "a dime a dozen," and IP only becomes defensible once a product has been built and executed successfully.

### **Rapid-Fire Insights**

The following table summarizes the personal preferences and tools endorsed by the Disclosy founder:

| Category | Response |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Tool to Swear By** | Host’s Rule (Internal protocol) |
| **Tool to Avoid** | Legacy Salesforce |
| **Favorite Book** | *The Seven Habits of Highly Effective People* |
| **Primary Metric** | Email open rates |
| **Motivation Factor** | Seeing users interact with the system as intended |
| **Success Secret** | Relying on a dependable development team |

## **Conclusion**

The overarching theme of the discussion is that entrepreneurship requires a shift from a "fun project" mindset to a disciplined, education-backed execution strategy. Success is predicated on the ability to manage the exhaustion of multi-role responsibilities, maintain a focus on sales and marketing, and prioritize strategic partnerships over mere feature accumulation.

# Episode 002

# **Briefing Document: Insights from Alberta Founders Podcast Episode 2**

## **Executive Summary**

This briefing document synthesizes key insights from the second episode of the *Alberta Founders Podcast*, featuring Tamara, an experienced technical designer and entrepreneur. Tamara is the founder of a specialized platform that automates the generation of technical "shop drawings" for the commercial fencing and gate industry.

The core takeaways from this discussion include:

* **Product Innovation:** The platform replaces hours of manual CAD (Computer-Aided Design) work with a simple form-based input system, democratizing access to professional technical drawings.  
* **The Founder’s Reality:** Entrepreneurship is characterized by a "human Swiss Army knife" approach, requiring founders to balance high-level innovation with "unglamorous" administrative tasks and difficult sales cycles.  
* **Market Misconceptions:** The "Field of Dreams" fallacy—the belief that building a product is sufficient to attract customers—is debunked. Success requires intentional funnel building and proven market traction.  
* **Resilience and Autonomy:** Founders must remain resistant to external skepticism and focus on solving tangible user problems through iterative development and disciplined execution.

\--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## **Business Model and Product Innovation**

### **Core Value Proposition**

Tamara’s business serves commercial fence companies and specifiers, such as architects and landscape architects. The platform automates the creation of technical drawings that traditionally required specialized CAD skills and significant time.

| Feature | Description |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Automation** | Converts form-based specifications into professional shop drawings. |
| **Efficiency** | Reduces drawing time from approximately four hours to roughly two minutes. |
| **Accessibility** | Eliminates the need for users to own, know, or learn CAD software. |
| **Comprehensive Output** | Generates drawing sheets complete with title blocks and specifications suitable for engineering submission. |

### **Technical Execution**

The platform’s backend is built on complex logic to handle the vast variability of fencing projects:

* **Scale and Scope:** Some generators incorporate hundreds of drawing options based on variables like height, width, and hardware combinations.  
* **Technology Stack:** The system utilizes PHP code to pull specific, one-to-one scale drawings from a pre-loaded database.  
* **Security and Indexing:** To protect intellectual property, the platform uses internal naming conventions (e.g., coded strings) to prevent drawings from being indexed or easily found via search engines.

\--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## **The Entrepreneurial Journey**

### **The "Multiple Hats" Challenge**

A primary difficulty in the early stages of a startup is prioritization. Tamara describes the necessity of being a "human Swiss Army knife," switching daily between roles:

* **Marketer:** Cold calling and brand awareness.  
* **Developer:** Writing custom code and managing technical infrastructure.  
* **Administrator:** Managing "boring" but essential tasks like filing and general admin.

### **Debunking Startup Myths**

The podcast highlights the discrepancy between popular media portrayals of startups and reality.

* **The "Weekend Success" Myth:** Tamara refutes the idea that apps are built in a weekend and gain 10,000 users by Monday.  
* **The Glamour Gap:** The reality of the founder's life is "unglamorous" and involves a "marathon" of daily discipline rather than the "private jets and Lamborghinis" often seen on social media.

### **Shiny Object Syndrome**

While Tamara admits to "shiny object syndrome"—the urge to start new projects—she emphasizes the importance of sticking to a core idea. Her current business began as a "shiny object" inspired by a legal form generator, leading to a prototype built in just three days.

\--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## **Sales, Marketing, and Traction**

### **The Difficulty of Sales**

Tamara identifies sales as the most challenging aspect of the business. Transitioning from a problem-solving mindset to a "forceful" sales mindset is a significant hurdle for many founders.

* **Key Insight:** "Building it" does not mean they will come. Marketing is required to ensure potential clients know the solution exists before they actually need it.  
* **Strategy:** Tamara utilizes sales training videos and books (e.g., Brian Tracy) to learn "closing" techniques and how to move prospects from "no" to "maybe" and eventually "yes."

### **Funding and Investor Relations**

A critical warning is issued regarding securing investment.

* **The "Idea" Trap:** The advice that one can secure funding based solely on a good idea is labeled as "BS."  
* **The Requirement for Traction:** Investors are human beings looking for a return on investment; they require proof of revenue or market traction before committing capital. A business must be "proven" before it is fundable.

\--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## **Founder Philosophy and Advice**

### **Persistence Against Skepticism**

Tamara encourages founders to ignore those who claim a goal is impossible. She cites her own experience as a published author without an English degree as evidence that traditional requirements and external doubts should not be barriers to entry.

* **Quote:** "Just because somebody tells you something's not possible that doesn't mean they're right."

### **Future Outlook**

The platform is moving toward becoming a comprehensive project management tool for the fencing industry.

* **User-Driven Development:** Future updates include drawing generators based on user requests.  
* **Expanded Documentation:** The platform will soon incorporate forms for change order requests, progress billing, and other administrative project management needs.

\--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## **Rapid Fire Insights**

* **Essential Tool:** **Later.com** (for social media automation).  
* **Tool to Avoid:** **PayPal** (described as a "dinosaur").  
* **Current Reading:** ***The Let Them Theory*** (focuses on letting go of the need to control others' perceptions).  
* **Key Business Metric:** **Google Analytics** (obsessive tracking of user behavior).  
* **Business Lifesaver:** **Customer Service**.  
* **Stress Relief:** High-intensity interval training (gym) and listening to "angry '90s rock music."

# Episode 003

# **Executive Briefing: The Entrepreneurial Journey of AJ Leece and the Gamification of Business Processes**

## **Executive Summary**

This briefing document synthesizes key insights from an interview with AJ Leece, the founder and managing director of **Briade (Business Resiliency Arcade)**. Leece’s venture occupies a unique intersection of business consulting, information security, and video game design. The central mission of the organization is to transform mundane or "boring" business requirements—initially focusing on cybersecurity foundational tasks—into engaging, accessible video games.

The core takeaways from Leece’s experience include:

* **The Power of Gamification:** Leveraging entertainment to increase the accessibility and perceived value of technical business processes.  
* **The Solopreneur Framework:** Operating as a "Hipster, Hacker, and Hustler" simultaneously, particularly in the bootstrapping phase.  
* **Problem-First Methodology:** The necessity of possessing deep domain knowledge to solve "million-dollar problems" rather than building generic tools.  
* **Operational Resilience:** Using automation and custom-built tools to mitigate the "mentally taxing" aspects of business management, such as content creation and scenario writing.

\--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## **Company Profile: Briade**

Briade is described by Leece as an "experiment" in workspace design, aimed at making work less "terrible" and more engaging.

| Category | Description |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Core Mission** | To design and build video games that make boring business tasks fun and entertaining while remaining accessible. |
| **Primary Sector** | A hybrid of business consulting, information security training, and video game development. |
| **Initial Focus** | Cybersecurity awareness and foundational security program tasks. |
| **Funding Status** | Bootstrapped and revenue-only; the founder serves as the sole "boss." |

\--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## **The Founder’s Roles: The Three Hats**

Leece highlights a framework for startup composition, noting that every startup requires three distinct personas. In his current stage, he must inhabit all three roles simultaneously:

1. **The Hipster:** Focused on the design, branding, and "fun" aesthetic of the product.  
2. **The Hacker:** Focused on technical feasibility, automation, and building the underlying product.  
3. **The Hustler:** Focused on sales, marketing, and the "hustle" required to find and retain customers.

Leece notes that context-switching between these roles is one of the most mentally exhausting aspects of being a founder. To manage this, he utilizes "rituals" like walking his dogs or performing simple, outcome-oriented chores (vacuuming or washing dishes) to reset his mind between tasks.

\--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## **Operational Challenges and Innovation**

### **Overcoming Content Creation Friction**

The most "hated" yet critical part of Leece’s business is writing game "events"—short narratives that intersect technical cyber scenarios with stand-up comedy and business applicability. To solve this, Leece developed a custom application using a "citizen developer suite" (prior to the advent of modern AI tools like ChatGPT).

* **Solution:** This mobile tool allows him to capture inspiration immediately—whether at a bar, getting an oil change, or on a train—reducing the friction of sitting down to write from scratch.

### **The Customer Retention Hump**

Leece observes a distinct "hype cycle" in business growth:

* **Phase 1 (Early Stages):** Finding customers was relatively simple due to personal networks and the novelty of the product.  
* **Phase 2 (Sustainability):** Retaining and finding new customers became "terribly difficult" once the initial hype died down and existing clients underwent structural changes (acquisitions or staff departures).

\--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## **Strategic Management of "Shiny Object Syndrome"**

While often viewed as a weakness, Leece argues that "Shiny Object Syndrome" is a "power that must be wielded responsibly."

* **Tactical Implementation:** Instead of ignoring new ideas, founders should evaluate them based on their stickiness and alignment with the core business vision.  
* **Evaluation Metrics:**  
  * **Market Size:** Is the new market vertical large enough?  
  * **Revenue Potential:** Can a "buck be wrapped around it"?  
  * **Customer Feedback:** Have potential buyers or mentors mentioned this specific problem in passing?  
* **The Side Quest Analogy:** In video game terms, while the "main quest" (core business) is vital, "side quests" can contain "good loot" (unexpected opportunities). The key is to ensure they do not consume the entire operation.

\--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## **Advice for Emerging Founders**

Leece provides several incisive recommendations for students and first-time entrepreneurs:

* **Cultivate Domain Knowledge:** Founders should get "super nerdy" about a specific problem they have personally encountered. Solving a problem without domain knowledge often leads to missing the nuances that drive sales.  
* **The X-Factor:** When selling to business decision-makers, a founder must be able to define why their tool is better than a billion-dollar incumbent's solution.  
* **Developing Intuition:** Founders must cultivate a "barometer" for advice. Leece warns that much advice comes from people with "no skin in the game." True intuition is built through the process of making mistakes and learning from them.  
* **Authenticity in Branding:** Leece advocates for leaning into a business’s true identity. If the business sells "fun," the founder should "look like they sell fun" rather than adhering to traditional corporate aesthetics.

\--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## **Future Outlook for Briade**

The organization is currently pruning secondary services to focus almost exclusively on video game development. Upcoming projects include:

* **Incident Response Games:** Tools to help companies test their response plans for cyber incidents outside of traditional boardrooms.  
* **Employee Onboarding:** Developing gamified processes to make new hire integration more engaging.  
* **Marketing Vehicles:** Designing games for other companies to use as marketing tools to showcase their brand personality.

### **Recommended Resource**

Leece recommends the book **"Rework"** (detailing the story of 37 Signals) as essential reading for founders to help them understand the type of business they want to grow.

# Episode 004

# **Briefing Document: Insights from the Alberta Founders Podcast – Nick Rogers (Robin)**

## **Executive Summary**

This document synthesizes key insights from an interview with Nick Rogers, co-founder of **Robin**, an AI-driven SMS platform for small and medium-sized businesses (SMBs). The analysis explores the intersection of Large Language Model (LLM) implementation, the operational realities of a startup founder in Alberta, and the strategic prioritization required to scale a technology company.

Critical takeaways include:

* **The Utility of "Frictionless" AI:** Robin leverages LLMs to automate repetitive customer service tasks via standard SMS, removing the barrier of specialized apps or web interfaces.  
* **The Hidden Complexity of Infrastructure:** While AI workflows are prominent, the most significant technical hurdles involve foundational systems such as Role-Based Access Control (RBAC), billing, and data siloing.  
* **Founder Mindset and Grit:** Success is attributed to "being where your feet are" (total presence), rigorous time-blocking—including intentional all-nighters for deep work—and a commitment to the local tech ecosystem despite the "talent drain" to Silicon Valley.  
* **Strategic Evolution:** The company is transitioning from simple automated messaging to "Agentic AI," which involves deeper tool integrations (e.g., calendars) and expansion into the United States market.

\--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## **I. Robin: Platform Overview and Value Proposition**

Robin is a year-old startup designed to provide SMBs with an automated, AI-powered communication channel.

* **Functionality:** The platform provides businesses with a dedicated phone number. Users text this number and receive immediate, automated responses powered by an LLM.  
* **Customization:** Businesses can define the "behavior, tone of voice, brand voice, and knowledge base" of the AI, effectively creating a "ChatGPT for your business."  
* **Target Use Cases:** The platform is particularly effective for large events where small teams would otherwise be overwhelmed by repetitive FAQs.  
* **Onboarding:** The primary advantage is its "frictionless" nature; customers interact through standard SMS without needing to navigate websites or download new applications.

\--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## **II. Technical Strategy and Implementation**

As the lead of technology, Rogers emphasizes a balance between cutting-edge experimentation and stable, "boring" infrastructure.

### **The "Hidden" Technical Challenges**

Contrary to popular belief, the advanced AI workflows—defining system prompts and toolsets—are not the most difficult parts of the build. The actual complexity lies in:

* **Organizational Structure:** Implementing robust RBAC, team invitations, and user permissions.  
* **Data Security:** Ensuring total siloing of data so information never leaks between different organizations.  
* **Billing Systems:** Developing complex, usage-based billing mechanisms.  
* **Security:** Rogers notes that while modern frameworks like React can obfuscate code, security must eventually become a full-time role to prevent intellectual property theft and data breaches.

### **Technology Stack Preferences**

* **Stable Foundations:** Rogers advocates for "boring technology" in the early stages, favoring a monolith architecture and a Postgress database over premature complexity like microservices, GraphQL, or Kubernetes.  
* **Experimentation:** To stay competitive in AI, the team conducts "small, measurable experiments" with new models without necessarily shipping every experiment to the live production environment.

\--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## **III. The Realities of the Founder Experience**

The document highlights the mental and operational friction inherent in building a startup from the ground up.

### **Operational "Fires" and Triage**

* **The Burden of Choice:** Founders face "private anguish" when they must triage issues. Not every bug can be fixed immediately; small issues (e.g., those affecting only internal admin users) must sometimes "burn" to prioritize mission-critical tasks.  
* **The Decision Gap:** Unlike being an employee with a defined "lane," a founder must decide every task and priority, often with limited runway and high uncertainty.

### **The Alberta Ecosystem Challenge**

* **Talent Drain:** A major frustration for local founders is the loss of talent to Silicon Valley (San Francisco) and New York, driven by higher salaries and more mature ecosystems.  
* **Commitment to Locality:** Rogers argues that building global companies in Alberta is possible and necessary to create opportunities that encourage the most intelligent peers to remain in the province.

\--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## **IV. Productivity and Personal Frameworks**

Rogers utilizes specific rituals and philosophies to maintain clarity amidst the chaos of entrepreneurship.

* **Deep Work All-Nighters:** Every Tuesday from 12:00 AM to 8:00 AM, Rogers performs an intentional all-nighter. This distraction-free block allows for high-output coding that can exceed a standard week's worth of interrupted work.  
* **"Be Where Your Feet Are":** A personal mantra focused on 100% presence. If in a meeting or a podcast, all other distractions (like Slack) are ignored to ensure the quality of the current interaction.  
* **The "Fire" Philosophy:** Drawing on the quote, *"In fire is where gold is tested,"* Rogers views the uncertainty and pressure of startup life as the necessary environment for creating something of value.

\--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## **V. Networking and Community Engagement**

Networking is reframed as "finding your people" rather than forced card exchanges.

* **Co-working Spaces:** Engagement with spaces like **House 831** in Calgary provides a community of builders and influencers that helps maintain accountability.  
* **Niche Interest Groups:** Participation in informal groups like "Bike Club"—a Saturday morning coffee meetup for developers to discuss code, mechanical keyboards, and brewing—provides a necessary social outlet for "geeking out."  
* **Teaching and Speaking:** Public speaking is highlighted as a dual-purpose tool: it provides company exposure and forces the speaker to understand their subject matter more deeply through the act of explaining it.

\--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## **VI. Quick Reference: Tools and Resources**

| Category | Item | Context/Notes |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Essential Tool** | **PostHog** | Highly recommended for product analytics ("The Goat"). |
| **Avoided Tool** | **Sublime Text** | Noted as a tool the founder would not return to. |
| **Recommended Reading** | *The Creative Act* | By Rick Rubin; focuses on the nature of creation. |
| **Key Metric** | **Sales & WAUs** | Focus on revenue and "Weekly Active Robins." |
| **Development Safety** | **Unit Tests** | Credited with saving the business multiple times. |

\--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## **VII. Future Outlook for Robin**

The roadmap for Robin indicates a shift toward more complex automation and geographical expansion:

1. **Year of the Agent:** Moving beyond text replies to "Agentic AI" that can perform actions.  
2. **Integrations:** Tightening links with existing tools, specifically Google Calendar and other user management systems.  
3. **U.S. Expansion:** Preparing for the first pilot with an American company, which involves navigating complex compliance and paperwork.  
4. **Market Growth:** Despite the product being "cheaper than it will ever be," the current focus is on user retention and proving high ROI for businesses before adjusting pricing.

# **Briefing Document: Insights from Alberta Founders Podcast — Jeremy Hunter (Omnio Inc.)**

## **Executive Summary**

This document synthesizes the core themes and strategic insights from an interview with Jeremy Hunter, the CEO and founder of Omnio Inc. Based in Calgary, Omnio focuses on a novel approach to vehicle security, prioritizing analog-focused protection over hackable wireless software. Hunter identifies several critical hurdles for modern founders, including the ambiguity of business "rules," the inherent difficulty of establishing trust within professional networks, and the high barrier to entry created by bureaucratic grant processes.

Key takeaways include:

* **Omnio’s Value Proposition:** Preventing car theft through non-wireless methods applicable to both legacy analog vehicles and modern software-integrated cars.  
* **The Problem of Trust:** The entrepreneurial landscape is characterized by "wolves in sheep's clothing," where identifying authentic mentors is difficult and early failures often come from being "burned" by others.  
* **Operational Discipline:** Strategies for managing "Shiny Object Syndrome" and "social battery" exhaustion through the "Saturday Jeremy" protocol and the externalization of ideas.  
* **IP Risks of AI:** A warning regarding the ownership of company documentation and branding created through AI tools, emphasizing the liability of not holding a contractual claim to core assets.

\--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## **Omnio Inc.: A Non-Wireless Approach to Vehicle Security**

Omnio Inc. was founded to address the rising issue of vehicle theft in Canada. Unlike traditional security systems that rely on wireless signals, Omnio utilizes a physical/analog-focused methodology.

* **Technology Gap:** Hunter argues that current wireless software—including Bluetooth, RFID, and cellular signals—makes vehicles vulnerable to hacking.  
* **Vehicle Compatibility:** The solution is designed to be versatile:  
  * **Legacy Vehicles:** Focuses on specific analog functions, such as the steering column and wheel weld connections.  
  * **Modern Vehicles:** Interfaces with systems primarily handled by software and wireless signals.  
* **Core Philosophy:** The project is driven by the belief that every individual deserves to feel and be safe in their vehicle without the risk of high-tech exploitation.

\--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## **The Entrepreneurial Landscape: Challenges and Realities**

### **The "Unwritten" Rules of Business**

Hunter describes entering the business world as a "test" without a study guide. He identifies a significant gap between the vast amount of generic information available and the specific, actionable knowledge required to succeed.

* **Ambiguity:** There are no clearly written rules for entering business; resources are often contrived or misleading.  
* **The Trust Deficit:** A central challenge is discerning the motives of others. Hunter notes the difficulty in distinguishing "wolves" from "sheep," particularly when dealing with "multi-time founders" who may use their history as a tool of intimidation or manipulation.

### **Bureaucracy and the Grant Process**

The process of securing funding through grants is highlighted as an unexpectedly exhausting facet of business.

* **Secret Language:** Grant applications are described as requiring a "secret password"—a specific colloquial or technical language that is not documented and often expires before it can be mastered.  
* **Bureaucratic Friction:** The system requires navigating numerous channels and personas, which adds layers of complexity to an already ambiguous process.

\--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## **Strategic Management of the Founder Mindset**

### **Managing "Shiny Object Syndrome"**

To prevent being overwhelmed by constant new ideas (such as improvements for shoelaces or medical canes), Hunter employs a system of "closure":

1. **Externalization:** Writing ideas down or using speech-to-text.  
2. **Social Sharing:** Telling others about the idea to give it "permanence."  
3. **Collaborative Delegation:** Passing ideas to those who can use them, such as connecting business sellers with buyers for a commission.

### **The "Saturday Jeremy" (SJ) Protocol**

Hunter manages social exhaustion and "low battery" days through a specific persona termed "Saturday Jeremy."

* **Purpose:** To signal a need for rest and a low-tolerance for "BS" or unnecessary social complexity.  
* **Communication Style:** Replies are strictly limited to the point, often marked as "SJ" or "Saturday Jeremy" to set expectations.  
* **Leadership Rule:** Even in this mode, Hunter follows a self-imposed rule of never replying with fewer than three words to maintain a standard of professional respect.

\--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## **Intellectual Property and AI Liability**

Hunter expresses significant skepticism regarding the overuse of AI in business documentation and branding.

* **Ownership Concerns:** If a company’s documentation is 60% AI-generated without a specific contract with the AI provider (e.g., OpenAI, Google, Microsoft), the company may not legally own its foundational materials.  
* **The AI "Signature":** Hunter shares an anecdote regarding his company logo. Though initially generated by AI, he discovered that AI-produced colors are not solid but vary pixel by pixel (a "signature"). To ensure absolute ownership, he remade the logo pixel-by-pixel in Microsoft Paint.

\--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## **Entrepreneurial Philosophy: The "Museum" Approach**

Rather than trying to "get over" or suppress negative business experiences (which Hunter likens to a tension-filled spring that can snap back), he advocates for a "Museum" approach:

* **Acknowledge, Respect, Honor:** Bad experiences, such as failed grants or betrayals, are placed on a "pedestal" in the mind.  
* **Purpose:** By treating these events as "art" to be observed rather than problems to be solved or forgotten, a founder can integrate them into their identity without ongoing emotional tension.  
* **"Love the Problem":** Hunter advises early-stage founders to focus on "loving the problem" rather than the solution. Passion for an inconvenience (e.g., a dripping coffee mug or people falling on the street) provides the fire necessary to sustain the journey through chaos.

\--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
