# Season 1 Episode 1

# **Alice Reimer and the Evolution of Fillip**

## **Executive Summary**

This briefing document synthesizes key insights from [Alice Reimer](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/alice-reimer), CEO of **Fillip** and a veteran of the Alberta technology ecosystem. The analysis explores the rigorous demands of the founder mindset, the digital transformation of the fleet fuel industry, and the systemic growth of the Canadian innovation landscape. Central to [Reimer](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/alice-reimer)’s philosophy is the belief that entrepreneurship is a team sport requiring deep resilience, strategic execution, and a commitment to mentorship. The document also highlights the perspective of investors like [James Lochrie](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/jameslochrie) on why founder quality remains the primary determinant for early-stage investment.

\--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## **1\. The Anatomy of the Founder Mindset**

[Alice Reimer](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/alice-reimer), an accidental entrepreneur who began her journey in 1999, describes the experience of founding a company as carrying an incredibly heavy backpack of obligation toward investors, family, and the community.

### **The Team Trifecta**

[Reimer](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/alice-reimer) emphasizes that successful startups are never solo ventures. She references the founding team trifecta originally proposed by [Dave McClure](https://www.linkedin.com/in/davemcclure):

* **The Hustler:** The external face of the business, responsible for uncovering opportunities, networking, and securing investment. [Reimer](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/alice-reimer) identifies herself in this role.  
* **The Designer:** Focused on the user experience and product aesthetics.  
* **The Hack:** The technical architect capable of building and scaling the technology stack (e.g., **Fillip**’s CTO, [Ted O’Grady](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/ogradyt)).

### **Key Philosophies on Starting a Venture**

* **The Value of Experience:** [Reimer](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/alice-reimer) advises against jumping into a startup immediately after university. She recommends working in established organizations first to learn the discipline of business, including collaboration, talent development, and individual contribution.  
* **Resilience vs. Flourishing:** While resilience is necessary to block out noise and adapt to setbacks, the ultimate goal is to flourish, using the pressure of the environment to fuel growth and outcome-focused execution.  
* **The Do or Die Moment:** In early 2021, **Fillip** faced a critical pivot point. [Reimer](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/alice-reimer) notes that the clarity of a go/no-go decision is actually calming because it forces a focus on the most essential outcomes.

\--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## **2\. Fillip: Disrupting the Fleet Fuel Industry**

**Fillip** is a fintech company focused on the digital transformation of fuel and maintenance cards for small fleet-based businesses.

### **The Problem: A 40-Year-Old Tech Stack**

The traditional fuel card industry relies on technology dating back to 1980-1985. The current reliance on plastic cards with magnetic stripes is increasingly incompatible with:

* **Energy Transition:** Plastic cards do not integrate well with EV charging infrastructure or hydrogen fueling needs.  
* **Consumer Expectations:** Modern drivers expect a mobile-first digital experience rather than antiquated physical hardware.

### **Strategic Growth and Market Acceptance**

Despite a non-linear path, including a pivot from B2C to B2B in early 2021, **Fillip** has achieved significant milestones:

* **Rapid Scaling:** Secured the first fleet fueling customer by November 2021\.  
* **Capital Raise:** Successfully raised $5 million in less than six months in a challenging market.  
* **Customer Engagement:** [Reimer](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/alice-reimer) maintains a hands-on approach, often joining the 24/7 support rotation to speak directly with customers and understand their business struggles.

\--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## **3\. Strengthening the Innovation Ecosystem**

Beyond **Fillip**, [Reimer](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/alice-reimer) has played a foundational role in building the infrastructure for Canadian innovation through **CDL Rockies** and **The 51**.

### **Creative Destruction Lab (CDL) Rockies**

Founded in 2017 at the **University of Calgary**, **CDL Rockies** aims to bridge the gap between invention (basic research) and innovation (commercialization).

* **Capital Raised:** Over $1 billion by participating companies.  
* **Equity Value Created:** $3 billion.  
* **Network:** One of 13 labs globally, focused on energy, health, and ag-tech.  
* **Core Thesis:** Providing judgment from experienced entrepreneurs to bridge the commercialization gap.

### **The 51 and Movement 51**

Co-founded with [Shelley Kuipers](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/shelleykuipers) and [Judy Fairburn](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/judy-fairburn-489ab530), this organization addresses the massive disparity in venture capital allocation.

* **The Wealth Gap:** Less than 5% of venture capital currently goes to women-led businesses, despite a projected trillion-dollar wealth transfer to women over the next decade.  
* **Movement 51:** A non-profit arm that demystifies early-stage investing through the Financial Feminist Investing Lab.  
* **Capital Impact:** **The 51** has raised multiple funds, including an Ag-food fund that saw a second close of $30 million, with a goal of exceeding $51 million.

\--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## **4\. The Investor Perspective: Why the Founder Comes First**

[James Lochrie](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/jameslochrie), Managing Partner at **Thin Air Labs**, provides insight into why investors prioritize the individual over the product in early-stage deals.

* **Evidence Scarcity:** At the seed stage, there is little data on market reception or pricing. Investors must have faith that the founder can figure it out when the original plan inevitably fails.  
* **Pinpoint Accuracy:** [Lochrie](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/jameslochrie) describes the do or die phase as a transition from a shotgun to a rifle, where the founder’s focus becomes exceptionally sharp.  
* **Self-Selection:** Entrepreneurs are often accidental or feel they don't fit the corporate status quo. Lochrie looks for founders who view their mission as a battle they have been trained to win.

\--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## **5\. The Alberta Advantage**

The source context highlights Alberta, specifically Calgary, as a premier destination for global innovation.

* **Human Capital:** The province boasts the highest-educated workforce and a culture built on an entrepreneurial mindset.  
* **Competitive Infrastructure:**  
  * **Research Institutions:** **University of Calgary** (Innovation District) and **University of Alberta**.  
  * **AI Leadership:** **Amii** (**Alberta Machine Intelligence Institute**) is ranked as one of the top AI/ML research institutes globally.  
  * **Public Support:** Initiatives like **Alberta Enterprise** and the **Opportunity Calgary Investment Fund** (**OCIF**) have made 25 investments over five years to diversify the economy.  
* **Livability:** Calgary is cited as the third most livable city in the world.

\--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## **6\. Critical Takeaways and Observations**

"The backpack and the obligation that you feel as a founder is big... but I don’t ever carry the backpack on my own. I can pull the bricks out and I can give them to other people", [Alice Reimer](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/alice-reimer)

* **Execution Over Ideas:** Success is not about the novelty of the idea but the discipline of execution.  
* **Non-Traditional Talent:** **Fillip** actively recruits non-traditional talent, such as retired firefighters or former dental practice managers, to bring diverse operational experience to their customer success teams.  
* **Mentorship as Fuel:** [Reimer](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/alice-reimer) views investing time in early-stage founders and women as a way to fill her cup while simultaneously diversifying the provincial economy.  
* **Market Opportunity:** The shift from Internal Combustion Engines (ICE) to EV and Hydrogen represents a multi-decade transition that Fillip aims to lead on a global scale.

# Season 1 Episode 2

# **Alex Todorovic and the Evolution of Arbor**

## **Executive Summary**

This briefing document analyzes the entrepreneurial journey of [Alex Todorovic](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/alex-todorovic-a3bb49135), co-founder and CEO of **Arbor**, a data science platform dedicated to product sustainability. The source context highlights [Todorovic](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/alex-todorovic-a3bb49135)’s transition from aircraft maintenance to technology leadership, driven by a desire for impact and a rejection of corporate inertia. Key takeaways include the importance of a resilient immigrant mindset, the strategic pivot from a consumer-facing app to a B2B data-centric model, and a unique approach to founder-investor relations. **Arbor**’s success is attributed to a highly technical founding team, a relentless pursuit of market validation through hackathons, and the timely capture of the growing global regulatory demand for Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) transparency.

\--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## **Founder Profile: Alex Todorovic**

[Todorovic](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/alex-todorovic-a3bb49135)’s path to entrepreneurship was defined by a shift from vocational trade to computer science, fueled by a realization that a traditional career path felt unfulfilling.

* **Background and Early Life:** A refugee from Bosnia who moved to Canada at age seven, [Todorovic](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/alex-todorovic-a3bb49135) attributes his work ethic to an immigrant mindset, a refusal to quit once resources have been committed.  
* **Career Pivot:** Originally trained in aircraft maintenance engineering at **SAIT**, he left the field after two years due to the internal panic of a stagnant future. He subsequently pursued a computer science degree at the **University of Calgary**.  
* **Motivation:** [Todorovic](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/alex-todorovic-a3bb49135) expresses a profound need for work that is not just a means to an end but a source of personal fulfillment and measurable global impact.

\--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## **The Genesis and Evolution of Arbor**

**Arbor** did not begin as a sustainability platform, it evolved through practice, competition, and rigorous testing of team dynamics.

### **From Practice to Product**

The founding team, [Alex Todorovic](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/alex-todorovic-a3bb49135), [Ben Grande](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/ben-grande-259499101) (co-founder), [Danny Grande](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/danny-grande), and [Abdullah Choudry](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/abdullah-choudhry-85b34ba7), initially incorporated a software services company for $150. This was intended as practice to learn hard business skills like financials and marketing before spinning out a specific product.

### **Key Milestones in Development**

| Milestone | Description | Result |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **ATP Hackathon (2020)** | Brainstormed an ESG-focused financial app called **Money Tree** in the early morning hours. | Awarded 2nd Place and a VR headset, validated team chemistry. |
| **Microsoft AI Competition** | A Canada-wide competition focused on sustainable economies. | 1st Place out of 400+ teams, confirmed significant market interest. |
| **Pre-Seed Funding** | Raised funds based on a consumer-facing browser extension. | Secured initial runway to test the concept. |

\--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## **Strategic Business Pivot: B2C to B2B**

A critical turning point for **Arbor** was the realization that their original business-to-consumer (B2C) model was economically unsustainable and lacked the desired scale of impact.

* **The Problem with B2C:** [Todorovic](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/alex-todorovic-a3bb49135) noted that a consumer app required excessive marketing capital to compete with established giants for consumer attention.  
* **The B2B Solution:** The team shifted focus to the source, businesses. By providing data to make companies more sustainable, they achieved a trickledown effect: if the business is sustainable, the products consumers buy are inherently better for the planet.  
* **Data-Centric Focus:** **Arbor** transformed into a data platform that helps behemoth-level companies measure and showcase product sustainability, moving beyond greenwashing to credible, measurable action.

\--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## **Operational Philosophy and Team Culture**

[Todorovic](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/alex-todorovic-a3bb49135) emphasizes that a startup's early hires and co-founder dynamics are the foundation of future leadership.

### **Hiring for the Startup Mindset**

[Todorovic](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/alex-todorovic-a3bb49135) distinguishes between two types of employees:

1. **Guided Workers:** Those who excel within a defined box and follow a consistent daily routine.  
2. **Malleable Workers:** Those who thrive in chaos and are willing to shift roles (e.g., from developer to sales). Early-stage startups require this latter group.

### **Co-Founder Dynamics**

Despite having four founders, a rarity in successful startups, the team maintains alignment through:

* **Radical Honesty:** A willingness to vent it out and address resentment immediately.  
* **Clear Decision-Making:** [Todorovic](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/alex-todorovic-a3bb49135) serves as the final decision-maker (CEO) to prevent 50/50 deadlocks.  
* **Shared North Star:** A collective belief that they can execute on anything as long as they remain unattached to specific ideas and responsive to data.

\--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## **The Investor Perspective: Thin Air Labs**

[James Lochrie](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/jameslochrie), Managing Partner at **Thin Air Labs**, provides an incisive look at why **Arbor** became a primary investment target.

* **Relentlessness as Table Stakes:** [Lochrie](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/jameslochrie) identifies [Alex](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/alex-todorovic-a3bb49135) as having the necessary missionary mindset and a competitive drive that is difficult to replicate.  
* **Humility and Equality:** [Lochrie](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/jameslochrie) was impressed by [Todorovic](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/alex-todorovic-a3bb49135)’s ability to treat investors as peers and resources rather than bosses.  
* **Market Timing:** The investment was a bet on the regulatory environment moving from voluntary early adoption to forced mass market transparency.  
* **Validation through The Impossible:** [Lochrie](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/jameslochrie) cites the team's ability to close deals under extreme circumstances, such as sleeping on a CTO's couch in Norway during the pandemic, as proof of their unstoppable energy.

\--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## **Entrepreneurial Insights and Hard Truths**

The sources provide several candid reflections on the reality of building a company from nothing.

"In this world, everyone loves you when you've got announcements to make. And when you're not making announcements, no one gives a shit about you. Get used to it", **Alex Todorovic**

* **The Fear of Failure:** [Todorovic](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/alex-todorovic-a3bb49135) admits to a recurring nightmare thought of having to tell investors their money is gone. He manages this by focusing on the value they have already built with global corporations.  
* **On Investor Relations:** Founders should treat investors like employees or resources. Being overly submissive (e.g., yes sir, sorry sir) undermines a CEO's perceived leadership ability.  
* **The End Game:** [Todorovic](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/alex-todorovic-a3bb49135) views the small scrappy journey as the most enjoyable part of the process. He expresses a desire to maintain control of **Arbor** to ensure the impact remains authentic and avoids "greenwashing marketing bullshit."

# Season 1 Episode 3

# **Nicole Janssen and the Evolution of AltaML**

## **Executive Summary**

This briefing document analyzes the entrepreneurial journey and strategic vision of [Nicole Janssen](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/nicole-janssen), co-founder and co-CEO of **AltaML**. [Janssen](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/nicole-janssen) describes entrepreneurship as an infectious disease, a relentless pursuit of self-actualization characterized by extreme financial risk and the sweetness of infrequent but high-impact wins. Central to her narrative is the belief that true entrepreneurship requires betting on yourself entirely, often through periods of intense personal and financial vulnerability.

Key takeaways include:

* **Operational Resilience:** High-stakes crises, such as the sudden withdrawal of a lead investor, underscore the necessity of a high-functioning Board of Directors and continuous investor relationship management.  
* **Leadership Through Constraint:** [Janssen](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/nicole-janssen)’s 2019 epilepsy diagnosis transformed her leadership style, forcing a transition from micro-management to radical delegation and a 50-hour weekly work limit, which she posits has made her a more effective executive.  
* **Strategic National Vision:** [Janssen](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/nicole-janssen) is a vocal advocate for Canada as a global leader in Responsible AI, arguing that Canada can secure this title by filling the ethical void left by the United States and China.  
* **The Venture Studio Model:** **AltaML** operates a dual-sided model, combining corporate AI lab services with a venture studio that spins off commercialized software ventures.

\--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## **1\. Entrepreneurial Origins and Philosophy**

[Janssen](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/nicole-janssen) identifies as a serial entrepreneur, a trajectory she traces back to a high school Junior Achievement program. This early experience in building a business, despite the triviality of the product (bear-shaped wooden piggy banks), instilled a permanent desire for autonomy and being in charge of \[one's\] destiny.

### **The Real Entrepreneurship**

[Janssen](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/nicole-janssen) adheres to a rigorous definition of entrepreneurship, stating that "you're not a real one unless you've mortgaged your house to make payroll."

* **Risk and Decision Making:** She argues that founders do not make their best decisions until they have navigated really hard times and high-stakes personal risks.  
* **The Win:** Despite the 90% terrible nature of the journey (a comparison she makes to parenting), the 10% of sweet wins provides an infectious motivation that makes returning to traditional employment impossible.  
* **Parental Influence:** [Janssen](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/nicole-janssen) was influenced by her mother, a forced entrepreneur who built a company after government funding for her employer ended. From her, [Janssen](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/nicole-janssen) learned the importance of grittiness, agility, and creating a collaborative culture.

\--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## **2\. AltaML: Business Model and Scaling**

**AltaML** is an artificial intelligence company focused on transforming data into actionable business solutions. It utilizes a distinct venture studio model.

### **The Two-Sided Model**

1. **Corporate Labs:** One side of the business collaborates with private and public sector enterprise organizations to move AI use cases from ideation to integrated solutions.  
2. **Venture Studio:** The other side identifies opportunities for commercialization, spinning off separate software ventures and supporting their growth.

### **Evolution of the Founder Role**

[Janssen](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/nicole-janssen) emphasizes that a founder’s role must change every six months to facilitate growth.

* **Delegation:** Transitioning from being the janitor, bookkeeper, and office manager to a CEO requires letting go of babies, tasks or projects the founder is personally attached to, such as the company culture handbook.  
* **Scaling through Others:** [Janssen](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/nicole-janssen) maintains that a company cannot improve if others are not allowed in to perform tasks differently than the founder might.

\--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## **3\. Crisis Management: The Portugal Incident**

A defining moment in **AltaML**’s history occurred while [Janssen](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/nicole-janssen) was on vacation in Portugal. A lead investor, under regulatory pressure to exit the tech sector, demanded the immediate return of their entire investment within a two-month window, just as the company was preparing for a new funding round.

### **Lessons in Resilience**

* **Board Utility:** The crisis highlighted the role of the Board of Directors. An exceptional Board acts as a highest performing team during crunch moments, providing strategic support and external networking to secure new capital.  
* **Constant Fundraising:** The experience taught [Janssen](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/nicole-janssen) that founders should have conversations with investors every day, rather than only when capital is needed. This maintains a rolodex of ready-to-go partners.  
* **Internal Composure:** [Janssen](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/nicole-janssen) notes the loneliness of leadership, where founders must project confidence to staff (No problem, we got this) while internally managing extreme fear.

\--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## **4\. Leadership and Personal Health**

In 2019, [Janssen](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/nicole-janssen) was diagnosed with epilepsy after years of misidentifying seizures as stress-induced panic attacks. This diagnosis fundamentally altered her approach to work and company culture.

### **Strategic Constraint**

* **The 50-Hour Rule:** Because her seizures are triggered by stress, lack of sleep, and alcohol, [Janssen](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/nicole-janssen) maintains a strict 50-hour work week, even including it in her contract with the Board.  
* **Efficiency:** The constraint of time has forced her to be more organized and focused. As investor [James Lochrie](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/jameslochrie) noted, constraint oftentimes makes entrepreneurs better.  
* **Culture of Wellness:** By being transparent about her diagnosis, [Janssen](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/nicole-janssen) encourages her team to prioritize their own health. She monitors staff for signs of overwork, arguing that employees provide their best work when they are healthy rather than grinding the midnight oil.

\--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## **5\. The Co-CEO Dynamic**

[Janssen](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/nicole-janssen) serves as co-CEO alongside her husband, [Corey](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/coryjanssen). While they initially vowed never to work together, they eventually realized **AltaML** required their combined, disparate skill sets.

### **Management Principles for Partners**

* **Stay Out of the Kitchen:** To avoid frustration, the two maintain strict boundaries regarding their respective areas of responsibility.  
* **Implicit Trust:** The success of the partnership relies on a foundation of trust that allows each to ignore how \[the other\] gets it there.  
* **Impact on Family:** Parenting teenagers while running a company together requires a high degree of presence and an effort to keep work frustrations from the dinner table.

\--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## **6\. The Underdog Vision: Canada’s AI Future**

[Janssen](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/nicole-janssen) views Canada as an underdog in the global AI landscape but sees a clear path to dominance.

### **Responsible AI Leadership**

* **The Global Gap:** [Janssen](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/nicole-janssen) argues that the US and China are unlikely to claim the title of global leader of responsible AI.  
* **Canada's Opportunity:** She believes Canada must get some swagger and seize this title through the collaboration of academia, industry, and government.  
* **Advocacy:** [Janssen](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/nicole-janssen) uses her platform to influence federal, provincial, and municipal levels of government, serving on boards like **Edmonton Unlimited** and **Mitacs** to drive the AI ecosystem forward.

### **Long-Term Goal**

Reflecting on a previous business sale to a US company, a decision she now believes was made due to a lack of confidence, [Janssen](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/nicole-janssen) is committed to keeping **AltaML** Canadian. Her goal is to build a billion-dollar company that grows wealth and talent within Canada, stating the company will remain in their hands until “cold dead hands”.

# **Darcy Tuer and the ZayZoon Journey**

## **Executive Summary**

This briefing document synthesizes the entrepreneurial journey and leadership philosophy of [Darcy Tuer](https://www.linkedin.com/in/darcytuer/), co-founder and CEO of **ZayZoon**. Drawing from his 23-year career as a multi-time founder, the analysis explores the evolution of a founder mindset, transitioning from accidental problem-solving to mission-driven leadership. Central to this narrative is **ZayZoon**’s 2021 liquidity crisis, which serves as a case study in relentlessness and the importance of founder-led investment during market uncertainty.

The document highlights [Tuer](https://www.linkedin.com/in/darcytuer/)’s focus on Earned Wage Access (EWA) as a tool for social impact, his structured approach to operational growth through Big Hairy Audacious Goals (BHAGs), and the critical role of the Calgary technology ecosystem in fostering innovation and economic diversification.

\--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## **The Evolution of the Founder Mindset**

[Darcy Tuer](https://www.linkedin.com/in/darcytuer/)’s entry into entrepreneurship was not driven by a master plan but by identifying a specific inefficiency within the oil and gas sector in 2000\. This initial pursuit evolved into a career defined by several key philosophical shifts.

### **Unplugging from The Matrix**

[Tuer](https://www.linkedin.com/in/darcytuer/) describes the transition from a traditional job to entrepreneurship as unplugging from the Matrix.

* **Paradigm Shift:** Once an individual successfully navigates the mechanics of business, such as managing payroll and invoicing, their perspective on the world changes. They begin to challenge the status quo and identify opportunities where others see only standard procedures.  
* **Freedom and Exposure:** This shift offers freedom for creativity but comes with significant exposure to risk, which [Tuer](https://www.linkedin.com/in/darcytuer/) notes is not suitable for everyone.

### **Embracing the Hurt Locker**

Entrepreneurship is characterized by an arduous journey that [Tuer](https://www.linkedin.com/in/darcytuer/) refers to as the hurt locker.

* **The Adrenaline of the Roller Coaster:** Founders must navigate extreme emotional highs and lows, often within the same day.  
* **Relentlessness as a Paramount Trait:** Success requires a willingness to face orders of magnitude more rejections (No’s) than Yes’s.  
* **Failure as an Asset:** [Tuer](https://www.linkedin.com/in/darcytuer/) argues that society often fails to embrace failure properly. In the startup world, failure provides the friction necessary to pivot and develop the resilience required for future success.

\--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## **Case Study: The 2021 ZayZoon Series A Crisis**

In 2021, **ZayZoon** faced a near-existential threat during its Series A fundraising round. Despite 10-12% month-over-month growth and clear product-market fit, the company nearly ran out of capital.

### **The Funding Gap**

* **Timing and Category Confusion:** During the summer of 2021, the Earned Wage Access (EWA) space was not yet a defined category for many institutional investors.  
* **The Rejection Cycle:** [Tuer](https://www.linkedin.com/in/darcytuer/) and his team pitched to 75 institutional investors and received 75 rejections. By December 2021, the company, then with 32 employees, was uncertain if it could meet payroll.  
* **The No More Angles Moment:** [Tuer](https://www.linkedin.com/in/darcytuer/) describes the agonizing period of staring at a blank whiteboard with co-founders [Tate Hackert](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/tate-hackert) and [Jamie Ha](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/jamie-ha-29bb6b135), attempting to find a path forward while keeping the stress confidential from the staff.

### **The Resolution: Leading the Round**

To save the company, the co-founders decided to lead the round themselves, committing their own personal resources.

* **Signaling Effect:** When the market saw that the founders were willing to invest their own capital to ensure the company thrived, it catalyzed broader interest.  
* **Outcome:** What began as a desperate attempt to raise 3 million turned into a 12.5 million equity raise, closed in January 2022\.  
* **Post-Crisis Growth:** Since closing that round, **ZayZoon** has quadrupled its business, grown to over 90 employees, and shifted to a position where it no longer needs to raise capital, which [Tuer](https://www.linkedin.com/in/darcytuer/) notes is the ideal time to do so.

\--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## **Mission, Impact, and The Why**

[Tuer](https://www.linkedin.com/in/darcytuer/) maintains that financial outcomes are a byproduct of solving a meaningful problem. His litmus test for any venture is whether he would be proud to tell his parents and children about the work.

### **ZayZoon’s Social Impact**

**ZayZoon** provides employees with liquidity on their already-earned wages to prevent them from falling into predatory loan cycles or bank overdraft fees.

| Metric | Target |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Big Hairy Audacious Goal (BHAG)** | Save 10 million people $10 billion. |
| **Daily Tracking** | Monitored via a **Metabase** dashboard during every morning scrum. |
| **Core Demographic** | Employees with credit scores in the 300-400 range who lack access to traditional financial products. |

### **Sustainable Philanthropy**

Following his time as Chairman of the **Missing Children Society**, [Tuer](https://www.linkedin.com/in/darcytuer/) committed to businesses with sustainable models that also provide a positive human impact. His portfolio includes:

* **ZayZoon:** Fintech for financial health.  
* **CATL:** Clean tech reducing particulates and environmental toxins.  
* **Aeon Future Health:** Focus on health and longevity.  
* **Tuesday Brewing:** A non-alcoholic beer company.

\--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## **Operational Strategy and Growth**

As **ZayZoon** transitioned from a startup to a scaleup, [Tuer](https://www.linkedin.com/in/darcytuer/)’s role shifted from a reactive fumbling to a coach for a specialized executive team.

* **90-Day Plans:** To prevent the BHAG from becoming overwhelming, the company operates on quarterly plans with two key initiatives.  
* **Quantitative Accountability:** Every team member must be able to state, at the end of the week, how they have quantitatively moved the company toward those objectives.  
* **Focus:** [Tuer](https://www.linkedin.com/in/darcytuer/) advocates for extreme focus, currently, 85% of **ZayZoon**’s energy is dedicated to the core mission of EWA, while using feedback loops to identify future financial products for their customer base.

\--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## **The Calgary Innovation Ecosystem**

[Tuer](https://www.linkedin.com/in/darcytuer/) and investor [James Lochrie](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/jameslochrie) emphasize the unique strengths and challenges of the Calgary and Alberta markets.

### **The Diversification Mandate**

* **Beyond Oil and Gas:** While the natural resource economy is currently performing well, [Tuer](https://www.linkedin.com/in/darcytuer/) argues that the future of the economy depends on diversification through technology.  
* **The Investment Challenge:** It is difficult to attract local capital to tech when traditional oil and gas investments offer high yields and rising stock prices. [Tuer](https://www.linkedin.com/in/darcytuer/) calls for deliberate seeding of local companies to ensure long-term job creation and talent retention.

**Gotcare** was established to deliver personalized, data-driven care in home settings, specializing in connecting rural and urban patients with home care workers.

# **Nivatha Balendra on the Development and Impact of Dispersa**

## **Executive Summary**

**Dispersa** is a Canadian clean-tech startup that specializes in converting food waste into sustainable, non-toxic chemical ingredients known as biosurfactants. Founded by [Nivatha Balendra](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/nivathabalendra), the company originated from a 17-year-old’s science fair project inspired by a local oil spill disaster. Since its official launch in 2019, **Dispersa** has raised over $14 million and transitioned its focus from industrial oil remediation to the consumer cleaning product sector.

The company’s core innovation lies in its proprietary Bioeterna process, which transforms waste oils and sugars into Purersurf, a waste-derived biosurfactant. This product serves as a biodegradable, petroleum-free alternative to traditional surfactants found in everyday items like detergents, cosmetics, and even food. Beyond technological innovation, **Dispersa** is defined by a mission-driven culture that prioritizes human health, environmental sustainability, and localized Canadian manufacturing.

\--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## **Origin and Founder Motivation**

The trajectory of **Dispersa** is rooted in a blend of early scientific curiosity and personal resilience.

* **The Catalyst:** In 2013, following a train crash and oil spill in Lac-Mégantic that released over 5 million gallons of oil, [Nivatha Balendra](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/nivathabalendra) began researching sustainable remediation methods.  
* **Scientific Discovery:** At age 17, for a school science fair, [Balendra](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/nivathabalendra) explored how certain microbes produce natural soapy ingredients called biosurfactants, which can break down pollutants like oil.  
* **Personal Turning Point:** In 2015, while a university student, [Balendra](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/nivathabalendra) was diagnosed with cancer. Her experience with chemotherapy and exposure to harsh chemicals in everyday products shifted her focus toward the health implications of ingredients. This second chance at life motivated her to ensure her scientific innovations contributed to the greater good.  
* **Entrepreneurial Shift:** While [Balendra](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/nivathabalendra) did not initially set out to be an entrepreneur, her drive to solve problems on a large scale led her to the cross-section of innovation and impact: entrepreneurship.

\--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## **Technological Innovation: Bioeterna and Purersurf**

**Dispersa**’s technology addresses the environmental and health concerns associated with traditional chemical surfactants, which are typically derived from petroleum or palm oil.

### **Core Technology Definitions**

| Term | Definition |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Biosurfactants** | Natural, soapy ingredients produced by microbes that are critical for products like laundry detergent, all-purpose cleaners, and cosmetics. |
| **Bioeterna** | The proprietary process developed by **Dispersa** to produce biosurfactants by upcycling food waste. |
| **Purersurf** | **Dispersa**’s flagship product; the world’s first fully waste-derived biosurfactant intended for the cleaning products sector. |

### **Key Product Features**

* **Feedstock:** Uses food waste, specifically oils and sugars, as the raw material.  
* **Sustainability Profile:** The ingredients are 100% biodegradable, non-toxic, and free from both palm oil and petroleum.  
* **Market Goal:** The company aims to provide sustainable ingredients that do not compromise on affordability or performance, making them accessible for mainstream use.

\--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## **Strategic Evolution and the COVID-19 Pivot**

**Dispersa**’s business model underwent a significant shift in response to the global pandemic.

* **Original Target Market:** The company initially focused on the oil and gas sector for environmental remediation, following **Balendra**’s original research.  
* **The Pandemic Pivot:** During COVID-19, the energy sector faced volatility while public focus shifted toward sanitation and cleanliness. Recognizing that surfactants are the primary cleaning agents in soaps and detergents, **Balendra** pivoted the company’s thesis overnight to cater to the cleaning products market.  
* **Outcome:** This decision required re-tooling applications testing and marketing materials but ultimately placed the company on a faster path to relevance and revenue.

\--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## **Organizational Growth and Culture**

**Dispersa** has expanded rapidly from a research project to a commercial-scale operation.

* **Funding and Scale:** The company has raised more than $14 million cumulatively. It currently employs 17 people (referred to internally as Dispersians), with plans to reach 20 by the end of the year.  
* **Leadership Recognition:** [Nivatha Balendra](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/nivathabalendra)’s leadership has earned her a spot on the Forbes 30 Under 30 list and the Governor General’s Innovation Award.  
* **Culture of Wellness:** [Balendra](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/nivathabalendra) emphasizes a work-life integration rather than a strict balance. She maintains a non-negotiable policy regarding weekends to prevent burnout, a value rooted in her perspective as a cancer survivor.  
* **Mission Alignment:** As the company grows from lab-scale to commercial production, leadership focuses on hiring mission-aligned individuals to ensure that the company’s core values are maintained at scale.

\--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## **Impact and Future Vision**

The company defines its success through a three-fold impact model:

1. **Environmental Impact:** By using food waste as a feedstock and creating biodegradable alternatives to petroleum-based chemicals, **Dispersa** reduces the carbon footprint of everyday consumer goods.  
2. **Human Health:** The company seeks to replace ingredients linked to hormonal imbalances, respiratory disorders, and toxic exposure (such as sulfates, parabens, phthalates, and 1,4-dioxane).  
3. **Economic Resilience:** **Dispersa** is committed to a localized Canadian supply chain. Its R\&D and product development are based in Laval, Quebec, while scale-up operations are located in Nova Scotia.

### **Long-Term Objectives**

The company intends to expand the application of Purersurf beyond cleaning products into multiple industries, potentially returning to the oil and gas sector for remediation. The ultimate goal is to leave the planet better for future generations by making sustainable living accessible through ingredient innovation.

# **Nanostics and the Evolution of Prostate Cancer Diagnostics**

## **Executive Summary**

**Nanostics** is a precision medicine company specializing in the development and commercialization of diagnostic tests, specifically targeting the early detection of clinically significant prostate cancer. Originally founded on a nanotechnology platform involving extracellular vesicles (EVs), the company underwent a critical strategic pivot after interim clinical data revealed that its hardware-based molecular test was more effective for late-stage cancer than the intended early-stage diagnosis.

Today, **Nanostics**’ lead product, Clarity DX Prostate, functions as a highly accurate machine learning algorithm, classified as software as a medical device. By integrating clinical data with standard-of-care inputs, the test provides a risk score that helps patients and physicians decide whether a biopsy is necessary. With over $19 million raised in combined equity and non-dilutive funding, **Nanostics** aims to eliminate unnecessary biopsies globally, potentially saving billions of dollars in healthcare costs while improving patient mental and physical well-being.

\--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## **Company Overview and Genesis**

**Nanostics** was officially launched in 2017, emerging from research conducted at the **University of Alberta**. The company’s trajectory was heavily influenced by a combination of philanthropic support and academic rigor.

* **The Catalyst:** The company’s mission was inspired by [Frank Sojonky](https://www.bowvalleyfuneral.ca/obituaries/frank-sojonky), a philanthropist who died from metastatic prostate cancer. Before his passing, he founded **The Bird Dogs**, a group that fundraised to establish a chair in prostate cancer research in Alberta, which eventually recruited Dr. [John Lewis](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/john-lewis-29301614).  
* **The Foundation:** The **Alberta Prostate Cancer Research Initiative** (**APCaRI**) served as the primary clinical research program. It recruited over 11,000 men and collected clinical data and samples from nearly 4,000, providing the massive dataset required to validate diagnostic tools.  
* **Leadership Structure:** The company operates on a complementary leadership dynamic between Co-founder and CEO Dr. [John Lewis](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/john-lewis-29301614), described as a visionary and manifestor, and Co-founder and COO [Catalina Vasquez](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/catalina-vasquez-994371b4), who provides the structured, goal-oriented operator framework necessary for execution.

\--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## **Technological Evolution: The Strategic Pivot**

**Nanostics**' name is derived from the integration of nanotechnology for diagnostics. However, the company’s core technology has evolved significantly from its original biological premise.

### **The Initial Platform (Extracellular Vesicles)**

The original research utilized super-potent microscopes and intravital microscopy to observe cancer cells shedding small particles (extracellular vesicles) as they exited blood vessels to cause metastasis. **Nanostics** partnered with a UK-based firm, **Apogee**, to use instruments originally designed for detecting biological weapons to identify these particles in human blood.

### **The Do or Die Moment**

During a clinical validation study involving a planned 2,800 patients, **Nanostics** conducted an interim analysis at the 1,600-patient mark. The results were a turning point for the company:

* **The Failure:** The extracellular vesicle (EV) portion of the test was effective for late-stage cancer but lacked the sensitivity required for early detection.  
* **The Discovery:** While the molecular test failed its primary objective, the machine learning algorithms developed by CTO Dr. [Robert Paproski](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/robert-paproski-9369a6141) were remarkably accurate at predicting risk using only clinical data and standard-of-care inputs.  
* **The Pivot:** The leadership team made the difficult decision to abandon the EV molecular portion and move forward with a software as a medical device model.

### **Clarity DX Prostate Today**

The current product utilizes a multi-algorithm approach:

1. **Clinical Algorithm:** Analyzes patient data such as age and history of previous negative biopsies.  
2. **Standard Inputs:** Integrates data from standard-of-care tests like total free PSA.  
3. **Risk Score:** A final algorithm combines these streams into a risk score indicating the likelihood of clinically significant prostate cancer.

\--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## **Clinical Validation and Regulatory Strategy**

**Nanostics** has prioritized rigorous validation to meet international standards and ensure clinical adoption.

* **Data Volume:** The test has been validated using data from approximately 3,500 patients.  
* **Diversity and Inclusion:** Following feedback from the FDA, the company expanded its data collection beyond its initial Caucasian-heavy Alberta cohort. **Nanostics** now collaborates with **UCLA**, **Johns Hopkins**, the **University of Florida**, and **Chesapeake Urology** to include African-American and Latin American patient data.  
* **Regulatory Path:** The company has submitted a request for Breakthrough Designation to the FDA and is preparing for a full pre-submission process.  
* **Funding:** **Nanostics** has raised:  
  * $10 million in equity funding.  
  * Close to $9 million in non-dilutive (grant) funding.

\--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## **Market Impact and Global Scalability**

The shift from a hardware/reagent-based test to a pure algorithm has fundamentally changed the company’s business model and potential impact.

### **Economic and Clinical Benefits**

* **Cost Reduction:** By removing the need for specialized instruments, reagents, and the transportation of biological samples, the cost of the test is dramatically lower.

## **Company Profile: Ambyint**

**Ambyint** specializes in accelerating the oil and gas sector’s progress toward sustainability through autonomous production optimization. While many tech firms in the sector focus on surveillance or anomaly detection, **Ambyint** distinguishes itself through true autonomous control.

### **Core Technology and Value Proposition**

* **Operational Scale:** The platform currently controls over 10,500 wells across North America.  
* **Target Market:** The platform focuses on the decline curve of a well. Once a well is no longer free-flowing, maintaining yield becomes a complex task. **Ambyint** automates this process for the approximately one million wells in North America currently utilizing lift systems.
